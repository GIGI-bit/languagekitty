"Kangaroo" you.

Everyone has a name. Each name has a meaning.Just like humans, animal species also have a name. But have you ever wondered why they have those names? For example, why are cats called cats? What about kangaroos? Why are they called kangaroos? It’s not proven scientifically but there’s a myth about how kangaroos were named.
According to the myth, these animals were named kangaroo for the first time in 1770 when on the banks of the Endeavour River, where a British Royal Navy research vessel (HMS Endeavour) beached for weeks to repair damage sustained on the Great Barrier Reef. The lieutenant James Cook - who was commanding the vessel, and the captain Sir Joseph Banks were exploring the area. Then they came across an animal. This was a 6 ft tall animal with a long tail which was jumping instead of walking. They watched this animal for a while and observed its actions. Curious to know what this creature was, they asked nearby locals about it. They asked it in English and obviously the locals didn’t understand. As a response locals said “kangaroo” meaning “I don’t understand” but Cook took it as the name of the creature.
Some anthropologists tried to correct this legend but, John B. H. was able to confirm that gangurru is a name of a large dark-coloured species of kangaroo. Although Phillip P. K. provided that locals use menuah to refer to a different species of kangaroos. There are still people who believe this legend.
